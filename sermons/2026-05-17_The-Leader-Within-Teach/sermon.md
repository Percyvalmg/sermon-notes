# THE LEADER WITHIN: TEACH
**Speaker:** Bert Pretorius
**Series:** The Leader Within — Making Disciples
**Date:** 2026-05-17
**Source:** 3C Live (3C Church, South Africa)

---

## INTRODUCTION

Continuing the series on the Great Commission — Matthew 28:18-20. Previous weeks covered *Go* and *Baptize.* Today: **Teach.**

**Key Text:** Matthew 28:19-20
> *"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you."*

Being a disciple is simply being an intern — someone who watches another's life and says, *"I'm learning so that I can become."* Paul frames it clearly:

**1 Corinthians 11:1**
> *"Imitate me as I imitate Christ."*

A disciple maker is not a spiritual specialist. It is a model. An example. Not something you do — **it is something you are.**

---

## POINT 1 — DISCIPLESHIP DOES NOT END WITH A DECISION, IT BEGINS WITH TEACHING

Salvation is the beginning, not the destination. Baptism is the first step of discipleship — it is obedience, it is doing. But **teaching is the process of transformation.**

Jesus did not say:
- Make converts
- Gather crowds
- Build attendance

Jesus said: **"Teach them."**

A decision without discipleship produces weak believers. Teaching produces mature followers of Christ.

The moment you receive Jesus and get baptized, you are only on the starting blocks. You haven't done anything yet. You haven't produced anything. You haven't yet fulfilled the purpose for which you are alive.

---

## POINT 2 — TEACHING IS ABOUT OBEDIENCE, NOT JUST INFORMATION

Notice what Jesus said in the commission:

> *"Teaching them to **observe** all that I have commanded."*

He did not say:
- Teach them to **know**
- Teach them to **discuss**
- Teach them to **agree**

He said: **teach them to obey.**

**John 8:31**
> *"If you abide in my word, you are my disciples indeed."*

**Information fills the mind. Obedience transforms a life.**

Disciples are not just informed — they are transformed. The word shapes their life. The word directs their living. The word produces obedience. True teaching produces obedience, and obedience is the evidence of discipleship.

**Obedience is not legalism. Obedience is love.**

**John 14:15**
> *"If you love me, keep my commandments."*

Obedience is proof that Christ is Lord. It is proof that faith is real. If you are not obeying, it means you either don't understand, don't want it, or don't yet have real faith.

---

## POINT 3 — DISCIPLESHIP IS A PROCESS, NOT AN EVENT

Teaching takes:
- **Time**
- **Relationship**
- **Consistency**

Jesus walked with His disciples. He spent time with them. Discipleship is not a class — **discipleship is relationship.** He taught them, He corrected them. You can't correct someone you don't know. You can't know someone you don't spend time with.

**Disciples are not made in a moment. They are formed over time.**

The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is today. The best time to disciple was 20 years ago. The second best time is now — not next week, not when you feel ready. God has already said do it.

**Hebrews 10:24**
> *"And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds."*

Discipleship requires intentional investment: your time, your money, your resources — into people. Not for a return, but because that is what it means to follow Christ.

---

## POINT 4 — TEACHING IS MODELING

**You cannot give what you do not live.**

We don't teach primarily with words. We teach with our lives — how we speak, how we lead, how we love, how we obey. **Your life is the loudest message.**

**2 Timothy 2:2**
> *"The things that you have heard from me among many witnesses, commit these to faithful men who will be able to teach others also."*

Paul doesn't just say what Timothy heard — he says what Timothy *witnessed.* His life was the evidence of what he taught. He says: now take what you have witnessed in me, and give it to faithful people who will teach others.

"Able to teach" does not mean the ability to speak. Paul himself says in 1 Corinthians 2: *"I did not come to you with excellence of speech or persuasive words of human wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power."*

Able to teach means **a demonstrated, transformed life.**

**Matthew 7:24**
> *"Whoever hears these sayings of mine and does them, I will liken him to a wise man who built his house on the rock."*

A wise builder is not one who merely hears — but one who does. Being a good pastor is not being a good preacher. Being a good pastor is **being a good model.** Some of the largest churches in the world are led by those who are not gifted speakers — but their lives, their marriages, their children are models worth following.

---

## POINT 5 — DISCIPLESHIP IS MULTIPLICATION, NOT ADDITION

Jesus' method was not addition — it was multiplication. He invested in 12. And those 12 reproduced what He gave them.

**When you disciple, you never have to worry about succession.** You have multiplied yourself into many — each one raising others to do the same. They no longer depend on you for your passing, because they have already become what you are.

**2 Timothy 2:2** shows the generational chain:

| Generation | Person |
|---|---|
| 1st | Paul |
| 2nd | Timothy |
| 3rd | Faithful men |
| 4th | Others |

That is depth.

Depth is not words — it's what you have produced. Depth is shown in your fruit. When your disciples can do what you do, and their disciples can do what they do, you have depth across generations.

> *"If we only gather, we add. If we disciple, we multiply."*

---

## POINT 6 — EVERY BELIEVER IS CALLED TO DISCIPLE OTHERS

Every believer — not just pastors, not just the spiritually advanced.

You are responsible for someone's growth:
- In your **home**, with your children
- In your **workplace**
- In your **neighborhood**
- In your **church**

That is the multiplication principle. The kingdom does not grow through platforms. It grows through one life investing in another.

---

## POINT 7 — TRUE DISCIPLES PRODUCE DISCIPLES

**A disciple who does not disciple is incomplete.**

Maturity is proven by reproduction. Even physically, maturity comes through the capacity to produce. You are not only called to grow — you are called to help others grow. That is how the kingdom expands.

---

## POINT 8 — TEACHING REQUIRES INTENTIONALITY

Disciples do not grow by accident. You must be:
- **Available**
- **Consistent**
- **Intentional**

The question you must ask yourself:

> *Who am I discipling? Who am I investing in? Who am I raising?*

If the answer is no one — that is what is missing. Not money. Not opportunity. Not the right season. The missing element is the core value of who God is: a creator who reproduces. And without it, there is an emptiness, because you are not doing what you were made for.

We don't just go. We don't just get baptized. **Our goal is to grow — and to help others grow.**

The Great Commission is not complete until disciples make disciples.

---

## CONCLUSION & CALL TO ACTION

**This is the hour where God is raising:**
- Strong disciples
- Mature believers
- Multiplying leaders

Not consumers, but contributors. Not spectators, but disciple makers.

The church is moving:
- From **gathering** → to **growing**
- From **growing** → to **multiplying**

**The declaration:**
> *"We are true disciples — abiding in the word, walking in obedience, living lives that reflect Christ. We will not remain immature. We will not stay as hearers only. We will become doers. We will use us to disciple others — wisdom to teach, grace to lead, boldness to invest. Disciples making disciples. Leaders raising leaders. Generations impacting generations."*

---

*Suggested closing exercise: Ask every person in the room to name one individual in their home, workplace, or neighborhood they will intentionally invest in — beginning this week.*
