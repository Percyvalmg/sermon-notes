# THE FATHERS OUR NATION NEEDS
**Speaker:** Bert Pretorius
**Date:** 2026-06-21
**Source:** 3C Church

---

## INTRODUCTION

Fatherhood is much bigger than you. It's much bigger than your own family. Fathering is a culture. That's why it takes a village to raise a child.

Just because you're male doesn't mean you're a man. When we're talking about man — man equals father. Whether you've got your own natural kids or not, it equals father. It's a culture. It's a responsibility. It's who you are. It's how we need to raise our young boys from a young age, understanding the responsibility they have.

**No one is self-made. We are all the product of fathering.**

> **1 Corinthians 11:1 (NKJV)**
> "Imitate me, just as I also imitate Christ."

Paul didn't say imitate Christ. He said imitate me. He gave the parameters — what you need to imitate is what's within him. He's saying *imitate me as I imitate Christ.* That's the parameters of imitation.

And all of us need to be able to say: **Imitate me. Be like me, act like me, speak like me, do like me.**

---

## POINT 1 — WE ARE THE PRODUCT OF FATHERS WHO WERE PRESENT

A father who conquers things in his life sets the next generation free from having to conquer the same things. **We're not looking for perfect fathers. We're looking for present fathers.**

A present father leads and guides. He places parameters within your life. He's somebody you can have a discussion with. You can disagree with. You can talk with — because **iron sharpens iron.**

Not a perfect father, but a present father. That's what changes a life.

---

## POINT 2 — SPIRITUAL FATHERS ARE ESSENTIAL, NOT OPTIONAL

> **1 Corinthians 4:15 (NKJV)**
> "For though you might have ten thousand instructors in Christ, yet you do not have many fathers; for in Christ Jesus I have begotten you through the gospel."

You've got 10,000 instructors. Everybody wants to tell you what to do. "10 steps how to succeed. 10 steps how to lose weight." But no one takes responsibility and says, "I'm going to walk with you and make sure you lose the weight."

**That's a father.**

Many instructions, but nobody in relationship with you who says: "I'm going to help you. Keep you accountable. I'm going to make sure that you serve God. I'm going to make sure you're successful. Whether you like it or not, I'm going to love you."

**No man is an island. No man is self-made.**

You need spiritual fathers who speak into your life. Who challenge you. Who are available. Who are present. Not perfect — but available.

Having pastors that love you, that care for you. **Any person that says "I'm self-made, I did it by myself" — that is a dangerous person.**

> **Proverbs 18:1 (paraphrased)**
> "He who isolates himself is a fool."

Never hang around a fool. A fool is a dangerous person. They're going to take you down a road where you're going to experience destruction eventually.

---

## POINT 3 — FATHERING IS A CULTURE, NOT JUST A POSITION

Fathering is bigger than just the men who walk closely with you. It's a spirit. It's a culture.

A godly man you meet for an hour can plant something in your life that lasts a lifetime. It's a "Hey young man, don't do that" kind of culture. You can either say, "Who do you think you are? I don't even know you." Or you can have a teachable spirit. You can learn and grow.

Today we celebrate fathers. We honor:
- Fathers
- Grandfathers
- Spiritual fathers
- Mentors
- Coaches
- Teachers
- Every man who has invested his life into the next generation

---

## POINT 4 — OUR NATION IS IN CRISIS AND NEEDS FATHERS TO RISE

As a nation, we stand at the crossroads in South Africa and we face enormous challenges:
- Crime and corruption
- Gender-based violence
- Family breakdown
- Unemployment
- Drug and alcohol abuse
- Hopelessness amongst our youth

While these problems have many causes, **one reality remains clear: strong families build strong societies. And strong fathers are the cornerstone of strong families.**

The absence of fathers is one of the greatest social crises of our time. Millions of kids growing up without the daily presence of a father. Some absent because of death, separation, or work. **But others are physically present but emotionally absent.**

The result is a generation that don't know who they are. Searching for identity. Searching to belong. Searching for purpose.

But today is not about condemnation. Today is a call. **A call for men to rise. A call for fathers to embrace your highest purpose.**

The future of South Africa will not only be determined by parliament and boardrooms and courts. It's also determined around dining room tables, in lounges and living rooms, on sports fields — every moment where a father chooses to invest into the next generation.

**What our nation needs are not perfect men. They need present men.** Men who love deeply. Men who lead wisely. Men who serve faithfully and leave a legacy that outlives them.

---

## POINT 5 — BEFORE YOU CAN FATHER, YOU MUST LEARN TO BE A SON

The foundation of great fatherhood is not simply a man who has children — it's a man who understands what it means to be a son.

**Before you can be a great dad, you must become a great son — a disciple of Jesus.** Discipleship teaches us how to be sons.

- You cannot teach submission if you're not submitted yourself.
- You cannot learn to honor if you don't honor yourself.

We learn how to be sons of our heavenly Father. And when we learn to do that, we learn how to become fathers to others. **God is the perfect Father.** Everything we know about fathering begins with him.

The quality of our fathering is directly connected to the quality of our relationship with our heavenly Father. **A good father is first a good son.**

How do we grow as good sons?

1. **Study God's word with the intention to obey.** Not studying for validation — "I'm good, I'm cool." You're studying to see: what do I need to do?

2. **Repent when you have gone down the wrong road.** A real man is not someone who never fails. A real man is someone who returns to God when he does.

3. **Allow God to build your character.** Grow.

4. **Commit daily to his ways.** You can never be proud. Never think you've got it together. Never think you've conquered. The devil is there waiting.

5. **Continue — repeat the process every day.** Discipleship is not an event. It is a lifestyle.

**Fess up where you mess up — and then fix up.**

Someone who is defensive is operating in the ugliest spirit. That's not a teachable spirit. That's an insecure spirit that will never grow. Even if 80% of what is said is not true but 20% is true — don't get worked up on the 80%. Take the 20% and say, "Yes, that's true. I need to change."

---

## POINT 6 — TEN PRACTICAL MARKS OF A GODLY FATHER

1. **They stick around.** Be available. Your presence matters.
2. **They respect the women in their lives.** Your children learn relationships by watching your relationship.
3. **They listen.** Children need to know that you heard them.
4. **They have fun.** Create memories. Laugh together.
5. **They show compassion.** Strength and tenderness can exist together. Be strong but be tender.
6. **They are tough but fair.** Boundaries create security.
7. **They model a strong work ethic.** Teach responsibility.
8. **They own their mistakes.** Humility always builds trust.
9. **They live with integrity.** Practice what you preach.
10. **They make their children feel protected.** You cover them. You fight for them. You pray for them. You stand for them.

---

## CONCLUSION & CALL TO ACTION

The call of God on you is bigger than your own household. God is calling fathers to rise up across South Africa to father communities, cities, and the next generation — to disciple young men, to mentor young fathers, to become builders of people.

**The crisis of our nation will not be solved merely by politics.** It will be solved when men return to God. When fathers return to their families. When spiritual fathers raise spiritual sons. When disciples make disciples — that's how a nation changes. That's how continents change.

The challenge to every father:
- **To your family** — renew your commitment. Be present.
- **To your children** — be intentional.
- **To your wife** — love sacrificially.
- **To the church** — serve faithfully.
- **To the community** — lead courageously.
- **To the nation** — take responsibility.

The future of South Africa needs fathers who will stand up and be counted. Not perfect men, but present men. Not flawless men, but faithful men. Not men who have all the answers, but men who know their Daddy, their Father.

Perhaps you have failed. Perhaps you have regrets. Perhaps you've never been taught or shown how to be a father. Today, God is not condemning you. **Today, He's calling you.** The Father is inviting you back to himself. When you discover your heavenly Father, you discover who He has created within you — the calling upon your life.

## SALVATION CALL

> **John 3:3 (NKJV)**
> "Unless a man is born again, he cannot enter the kingdom of God."

You cannot call yourself a child of God if you're not born of God. Coming to church doesn't make you a Christian. You must be born again.

What does that mean? You repent. Repent means to turn away from your own life. Say, "I'm done being my own God. I'm done. I need God in my life." And when you come to Him, He'll forgive you. He'll cleanse you. He'll change you. He'll make you brand new — give you a new start in your life.

> **2 Corinthians 6:2 (paraphrased)**
> "Today is the day of salvation."

Not later. Today.
