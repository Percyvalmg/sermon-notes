# THE LEADER WITHIN: GO
**Speaker:** Bert Pretorius
**Series:** The Leader Within — Making Disciples
**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Source:** 3C Live (3C Church, South Africa)

---

## INTRODUCTION (3 min)

Two-thirds of the name "God" is **Go**.

**Key Text:** Matthew 28:18-20
> *"All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you."*

Notice the order: **authority first, then go.** Jesus doesn't say "go because you're ready." He says "go because I am Lord."

---

## POINT 1 — GO STARTS WITH LORDSHIP, NOT MOVEMENT (5 min)

Before Jesus issues the command to go, He establishes His lordship. *All authority has been given to Me.* That word "therefore" is the hinge. Because He is Lord — therefore go.

We don't go when it's convenient. We don't go when we feel equipped. We go because He is Lord, and His command is not optional — it is obedience.

Many people want Jesus as Savior but not as Lord. But the Bible never separates the two. Every time Scripture calls us to receive Christ, Lord comes first.

**Romans 10:9**
> *"If you declare with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved."*

**Luke 6:46**
> *"Why do you call me 'Lord, Lord,' and do not do what I say?"*

The young rich ruler came to Jesus keeping every religious rule — but when Jesus said "sell everything and follow Me," he walked away. He wanted salvation without surrender. He wanted Jesus as advisor, not Lord.

**The moment you truly acknowledge His lordship, movement begins.**

When Paul met Jesus on the road to Damascus, blinded and on the ground, after asking "Who are you, Lord?" his immediate response was surrender:

**Acts 22:10**
> *"What shall I do, Lord?"*

That is lordship. Not fitting Jesus into your schedule. Not using Him like a vending machine — three healings, five prosperities, ten forgivenesses. When He is Lord, the question changes from *"what can I get?"* to *"what do You want?"*

**Psalm 23:1**
> *"The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want."*

When you know your shepherd, you shall not want — because you trust that as you seek first His kingdom, all things will be added.

---

## POINT 2 — GO IS THE RESPONSIBILITY OF EVERY BELIEVER (4 min)

This commission was not issued to pastors, missionaries, or the spiritually elite. It was given to all who follow Jesus.

**Mark 16:15** *(part of the longer ending of Mark, absent from earliest manuscripts — the commission in Matthew 28:18-20 is the stronger textual anchor)*
> *"He said to them, 'Go into all the world and preach the gospel to all creation.'"*

Everyone is watching someone. Your spouse. Your children. Your colleagues. Your neighbors. The question is never *whether* you're leading — it's *who you're leading for.*

**If you are saved, you are sent.**

You are not called to attend church and be blessed and go home. You are called to go, influence, and disciple. Every believer is a leader of influence. That is not a title — it is an identity.

**1 Peter 2:9**
> *"But you are a chosen people, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, God's special possession, that you may declare the praises of him who called you out of darkness into his wonderful light."*

You were called out — so you could go back in. Into your home. Your street. Your office. Your nation.

---

## POINT 3 — GO REQUIRES LEAVING SOMETHING BEHIND (6 min)

You cannot move forward while holding onto what God told you to release.

Abraham is the model. God said:

**Genesis 12:1**
> *"Go from your country, your people and your father's household to the land I will show you."*

He left his country, his relatives, his comfort zone — everything familiar. He didn't know the destination. He only knew the direction: away from self, toward God.

The disciples left their nets immediately when Jesus called — **Matthew 4:20** — *"At once they left their nets and followed him."* No negotiation. No timeline. Immediate obedience.

The young rich ruler couldn't do it — and missed his moment with God.

What must you leave?

- **Comfort** — the life designed around your own ease
- **Control** — the need to know every step before you take one
- **Fear** — fear of people, fear of failure, fear of looking foolish
- **Self-dependence** — trusting your own logic more than God's word

**Hebrews 12:1**
> *"Since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses, let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles, and let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us."*

Lay aside every weight. Not just sin — **weight**. Things that aren't necessarily wrong, but they're slowing you down. Opinions. Comfort. The need for certainty.

**Many are waiting for clarity. But God is waiting for surrender.**

---

## POINT 4 — GO REQUIRES FAITH, NOT FULL UNDERSTANDING (5 min)

God told Abraham to go to "a place I will show you." Not "a place I will describe to you." Not "a place I will give you coordinates for." *A place I will show you — as you go.*

**Hebrews 11:8**
> *"By faith Abraham, when called to go to a place he would later receive as his inheritance, obeyed and went, even though he did not know where he was going."*

He obeyed — then received understanding. Not the other way around.

Noah built the ark before anyone on earth had ever seen rain. Nobody knew what rain was. He built anyway — **Hebrews 11:7**. That is faith.

Peter stepped out of the boat onto water. He didn't know if it would hold him. He stepped anyway — **Matthew 14:29**. That is faith.

**Faith is not knowing everything. Faith is obeying anyway.**

**Proverbs 3:5-6**
> *"Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight."*

We are control freaks by nature. We want to map every step. But God says: *just go. I'll show you the place.*

Stop waiting for the full picture before you move. Move, and the picture will come into focus.

---

## POINT 5 — GO BEGINS WHERE YOU ARE (4 min)

We romanticize "going." We think it means a mission trip to another country, or a platform to preach from. But go begins in your home. Go begins in your workplace. Go begins in your neighborhood — right now, today.

**Acts 1:8**
> *"But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth."*

Notice the sequence: Jerusalem first. That was home. Start there.

If God wanted someone else to reach your workplace, He would have placed someone else there — not you. You are not at your job just for a paycheck. You are there because God has called you to touch the people in that building. You are the pastor of your floor. You are the light in that office.

**Matthew 5:14-16**
> *"You are the light of the world. A town built on a hill cannot be hidden. Neither do people light a lamp and put it under a bowl... let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven."*

Going is a **lifestyle**, not an event. It's something you **are**, not something you occasionally **do.**

---

## POINT 6 — GO ACTIVATES HEAVEN'S POWER (3 min)

Many people are waiting for power before they move. But power follows obedience — not intention.

The authority is already given. The power is already available. But it is released within the parameters of **assignment**.

**Luke 10:19**
> *"I have given you authority to trample on snakes and scorpions and to overcome all the power of the enemy; nothing will harm you."*

That authority is activated in the sphere of your calling. As a parent raising disciples — the power is available to take authority over sickness, over fear, over every attack on your family. As a believer in your workplace — the spiritual authority is released as you operate in assignment, not just self-interest.

When you go in obedience:
- When you speak, God moves
- When you step, He backs you
- When you obey, heaven responds

**Miracles don't follow intention. They follow obedience.**

---

## CONCLUSION & CALL TO ACTION (3 min)

**James 1:22**
> *"Do not merely listen to the word, and so deceive yourselves. Do what it says."*

We are not called to be hearers only. We are sent ones. We are disciple makers. We are not just receivers — we are leaders of influence in every space God has placed us.

The church cannot afford to be silent. Silence is not neutrality — silence is disobedience. The gospel was never meant to stay inside these walls.

**So today — make a decision:**

- Acknowledge His lordship completely
- Leave behind whatever He's asking you to release
- Step forward without waiting for the full map
- Start where you are — home, work, neighborhood
- Trust that as you go, His power activates

**Romans 1:16**
> *"For I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God that brings salvation to everyone who believes."*

You are the answer for your community. You are not here by accident. You are the right person, in the right place, at the right time — carrying nations inside of you.

**Go.**
