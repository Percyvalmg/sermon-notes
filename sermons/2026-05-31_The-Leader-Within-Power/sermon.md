# THE LEADER WITHIN: POWER — Lo, I Am With You Always
**Speaker:** Bert Pretorius
**Series:** The Leader Within — Making Disciples (Part 9)
**Date:** 2026-05-31
**Source:** 3C Live (3C Church, South Africa)

---

## INTRODUCTION

Concluding the series on the Great Commission — Matthew 28:16-20. This is the final portion of verse 20: the last words Jesus spoke before His ascension.

The commission is the framework within which every Christian conquers — in marriage, with children, as citizens, in the nation. Obedience to Jesus' instruction is what produces growth. Every Christian is a leader — someone is watching, someone is listening. The question is not *whether* you lead, but *how* you lead.

**Recap of the series:**
- Every Christian is called to make disciples — to be a model of godly living
- A disciple is an intern who follows and learns
- "Teach them to obey" means model — live what you speak
- Discipleship is not about perfection, but about how we respond to failure
- It is not condescending or judgmental — it is love

**Key Text:** Matthew 28:20b
> *"Lo, I am with you always, even to the end of the age. Amen."*

---

## POINT 1 — THE GREAT COMMISSION ENDS WITH A GREAT PROMISE

Jesus ends with a promise — and that is what makes the mission possible.

If we miss this, we will burn out trying to do God's work in human strength. It is impossible to make disciples and be a godly leader without God.

Jesus does not send us alone. The phrase **"I am"** is emphatic — God Himself, the great I AM, is saying: *I myself am with you.*

After He said go, make disciples, baptize, teach — He says: **"Lo, I am with you always."**

Why? Because Jesus knew:
- The mission would be difficult
- The opposition would be real
- The pressure would be intense

He wanted every disciple to know: **you don't have to do it alone.**

---

## POINT 2 — THE POWER OF HIS PRESENCE

The greatest promise is not influence, success, popularity, or comfort. The greatest promise is **His presence.**

Moses understood this:

**Exodus 33:15**
> *"If Your presence does not go with us, do not bring us up from here."*

The declaration: **"Lord, if You're not there, I don't want to be there."**

One moment of God's presence is greater than:
- Human strength
- Human wisdom
- Human ability

**The power of the church has never been in programs. The power of the church is in the presence of our Lord Jesus Christ.**

---

## POINT 3 — "LO" — PAY ATTENTION

"Lo" means: **pay attention. Listen carefully. Don't miss this.**

As a disciple you will face:
- Persecution
- Resistance
- Hardship
- Fear

Jesus says: "Hey — get this. You cannot miss this." He doesn't casually say "I'm with you." He says: **"Lo."** Stop. Pay attention. This is critical.

---

## POINT 4 — HIS PRESENCE GIVES US POWER TO OBEY

The Great Commission is impossible in human strength.

How do ordinary people reach nations? How do ordinary people make disciples? How do ordinary people stand against darkness?

**Zechariah 4:6**
> *"Not by might, nor by power, but by My Spirit, says the Lord."*

The command is big — because the promise is bigger.

---

## POINT 5 — HE DOES NOT SEND US WITHOUT AUTHORITY

**Matthew 28:18**
> *"All authority has been given to Me."*

That is why He used the word "therefore." He declared: I have all authority. This means:
- Hell does not have the final authority
- Fear does not have the final authority
- The world does not have the final authority

**Jesus does.** And when He sends us, we go under His authority.

---

## POINT 6 — THE HOLY SPIRIT IS THE POWER FOR THE MISSION

Jesus promised His presence through the Holy Spirit.

**Acts 1:8**
> *"But you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you."*

The Holy Spirit gives:
- **Boldness**
- **Strength**
- **Wisdom**
- **Courage**
- **Conviction**

Peter — who denied Jesus before a servant girl — became fearless once the power of God came upon him.

**Presence produces power. Presence produces power. Presence produces power.**

---

## POINT 7 — GOD'S PRESENCE SUSTAINS US THROUGH EVERY SEASON

Jesus did not say, "I will be with you sometimes." He said **always.**

Always means:
- All the days — not some days
- Not good days only
- Not easy days only
- **All the days**

**Hebrews 13:5**
> *"I will never leave you nor forsake you."*

You might feel alone. You might feel tired. You might feel pressured. But His promise still stands: **I am with you.**

---

## POINT 8 — HIS PRESENCE IS UNTIL THE END OF THE AGE

It is not temporary. It is for every generation.
- The early church needed it
- We need it today
- The next generation will need it tomorrow

The mission continues — and so does the presence of God.

---

## POINT 9 — WHEN HIS PRESENCE IS WITH US, WE CANNOT STAY PASSIVE

The presence of Jesus is not given for comfort only. **It is given for mission.**

His presence empowers movement.

**Acts 17:28**
> *"In Him we live and move and have our being."*

**Isaiah 6:8**
> *"Here am I, Lord. Send me."*

A true encounter with Jesus always brings:
- Surrender
- Boldness
- Obedience

It is the presence of God that makes the difference.

---

## POINT 10 — WE NEED HIS PRESENCE MORE THAN METHODS

You need His presence more than:
- Your processes
- Your systems
- Your structures
- Your organizational skills
- Your personality
- Your education

In the modern church we have systems, technology, excellence, and strategy. But **it is not the system that transforms a life — it is the presence of God.**

If we lose His presence, we lose everything.

- Programs cannot transform hearts — only the presence of God can
- The world does not need another performance
- Good music, good media, good production mean nothing without the presence of God

**What the church needs is to be filled with the Holy Spirit. What the church needs is the fire of God burning from the inside out.**

The presence of Jesus is the power of the church.

---

## PRACTICAL APPLICATION

When facing life's challenges — meetings, negotiations, crises, parenting, marriage, finances, national issues — the instruction is:

1. Get into the presence of the Lord
2. Get into the Word and the promises of God
3. Turn off distractions (radio, social media, apps)
4. Walk into situations with the fire of God, the peace of God, the joy of God

**"Lo, I am with you always"** applies to:
- Raising and disciplining children
- Dealing with your spouse
- Impossible marriages
- Financial struggles
- Workplace battles
- The nation of South Africa

Don't handle life out of emotion or intuition. God gives boldness, conviction, and strength.

---

## CONCLUSION & DECLARATION

The Great Commission is impossible without His presence. But with Jesus:
- Fear loses its grip
- Weakness becomes strength
- Ordinary people change nations

God is calling the church back to:
- **Dependence on God**
- **Intimacy with Jesus**
- **Boldness for the mission**

We cannot fulfill a supernatural mission with natural strength. We need His presence, His power, His fire.

**The Declaration:**
> *"Father, in the name of Jesus, we thank You for Your promise — I will be with you always. Today we declare: we will not walk in fear. We will not rely on human strength. We will not shrink back from Your calling. Fill us afresh with Your Holy Spirit. Let Your presence strengthen us, lead us, sustain us. We declare that we are not alone. Jesus is with us. His authority is over us. His Spirit is within us. We will go boldly. We will make disciples. We will baptize them. We will teach them to obey. We will stand firm until the end of the age. In Jesus' name. Amen."*

## SALVATION CALL

**John 3:3**
> *"Unless a man is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God."*

You do not have access to the presence of God unless you are a child of God. Coming to church doesn't make you Christian. You must be born again.

**What to do:** Repent — turn your back on your old ways. Turn to God. Invite Him into your life. He will change and transform you.

You can modify behavior through discipline, but you cannot change your nature. Only God can do that.

**The Salvation Prayer:**
> *"Dear Jesus, I need You in my life. Please forgive my sin. Take out this old nature. Fill me with Your Spirit. Everything that I am, I give to You. My whole life, I surrender unto You. Change my heart, Lord. Everything that I am is now Yours. I trust You, I trust Your word. It says if I believe, I receive You. I now have the right to be called the child of God. Thank You, Lord. As from now I belong to You. And nothing can snatch me out of Your hands because I am Yours. In Jesus' name. Amen."*
